\n

Reading and Viewing Level 10 Achievement Standard

\n

By the end of Level 8

\n

Students understand how the selection of text structures is influenced by the selection of language mode and how this varies for different purposes and audiences.

\n

They explain how language features, images and vocabulary are used to represent different ideas and issues in texts.

\n

They interpret texts, questioning the reliability of sources of ideas and information.

\n

They select evidence from the text to show how events, situations and people can be represented from different viewpoints.

\n

By the end of Level 9

\n

Students analyse the ways that text structures can be manipulated for effect. (1)

\n

They analyse and explain how images, vocabulary choices and language features distinguish the work of individual authors. (2)

\n

They evaluate and integrate ideas and information from texts to form their own interpretations. (3)

\n

They select evidence from the text to analyse and explain how language choices and conventions are used to influence an audience. (4)

\n

By the end of Level 10

\n

Students evaluate how text structures can be used in innovative ways by different authors.

\n

They explain how the choice of language features, images and vocabulary contributes to the development of individual style.

\n

They develop and justify their own interpretations of texts.

\n

They evaluate other interpretations, analysing the evidence used to support them.

Writing Level 10 Achievement Standard

\n

Students understand how the selection of language features can be used for particular purposes and effects.

\n

They explain the effectiveness of language choices they use to influence the audience.

\n

Through combining ideas, images and language features from other texts students show how ideas can be expressed in new ways.

\n

They create texts for different purposes selecting language to influence audience response.

\n

When creating and editing texts for specific effects, they take into account intended purposes and the needs and interests of audiences.

\n

They demonstrate understanding of grammar, select vocabulary for effect and use accurate spelling and punctuation.

\n

Students understand how to use a variety of language features to create different levels of meaning. (5)

\n

They understand how interpretations can vary by comparing their responses to texts to the responses of others. (6)

\n

In creating texts students demonstrate how manipulating language features and images can create innovative texts. (7)

\n

They create texts that respond to issues interpreting and integrating ideas from other texts. (8)

\n

They edit for effect, selecting vocabulary and grammar that contribute to the precision and persuasiveness of texts and using accurate spelling and punctuation. (9)

\n

Students show how the selection of language features can achieve precision and stylistic effect.

\n

They explain different viewpoints, attitudes and perspectives through the development of cohesive and logical arguments.

\n

They develop their own style by experimenting with language features, stylistic devices, text structures and images.

\n

They create a wide range of texts to articulate complex ideas.

\n

They demonstrate understanding of grammar, vary vocabulary choices for impact, and accurately use spelling and punctuation when creating and editing texts.

Speaking and Listening Level 10 Achievement Standard

\n

Students listen for and identify different emphases in texts, using that understanding to elaborate upon discussions.

They understand how the selection of language features can be used for particular purposes and effects.

\n

They explain the effectiveness of language choices they use to influence the audience.
